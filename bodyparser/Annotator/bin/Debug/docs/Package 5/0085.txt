ADDENDUM :
The patient was extubated overnight without incident .
He had a good cardiac index off all inotropic medications and his Swan-Ganz catheter was discontinued .
The patient underwent an exchange peritoneal dialysis .
The Parrack Diabetes Center was consulted to help manage the patient 's hyperglycemia .
By postoperative day two , the patient had remained in sinus rhythm and his Lopressor was increased .
He was having a bit of a respiratory acidosis , therefore , bicarbonate was added to the dialysate .
His mediastinal chest tubes were removed , however , his pleural chest tubes remained in place .
On postoperative day three , it was apparent that , despite an oxygen saturation of 99% on three liters , that the patient was still in a respiratory acidosis , and he received aggressive pulmonary toilette .
His pleural chest tubes were removed .
On postoperative day four , the patient Cordis was removed .
His Lopressor was increased another increment .
The patient was subsequently transferred to the floor on postoperative day five in stable and good condition .
Once on the floor , the patient had an uneventful hospital course , remaining afebrile for the remainder of his hospital stay .
The patient 's only issue was his hypertension .
On postoperative day seven , the patient had a peak blood pressure of 210/100 .
The decision was made to start the patient on lisinopril , and the initial dose of 7.5 mg was increased to 10 mg daily by the day of discharge in order to control his pressure more tightly .
On postoperative day seven , the patient was refusing to be discharged to a rehabilitation facility despite his doctor 's insistence that he would do much better in an assisted environment .
Nevertheless , the patient was adamant about going home .
The patient was discharged on postoperative day seven and arrangements were made to have physical therapy perform a home safety evaluation for the patient , to which he agreed .
